## Introduction

Sewer camera inspections help Miami homeowners avoid costly repairs. This technology lets plumbers see inside your pipes without digging up your yard.

**Why does this matter?** Finding problems early saves you money. Small issues like clogs or tiny leaks can turn into big, expensive repairs if ignored.

Miami homes face unique challenges:

- **Humid weather** causes pipes to wear out faster
- **Tree roots** often grow into sewer lines
- **Older buildings** have aging plumbing systems

Regular [sewer camera inspections](/services/camera-inspection/) catch these problems before they get worse.

## What Is a Sewer Camera Inspection?

A sewer camera inspection uses a small HD camera to look inside your pipes. The camera sits on a flexible rod that bends around corners. This method is safe and doesn't damage your property.

### How the Technology Works

- **HD camera on a flexible rod** - The camera moves through tight spaces and around bends
- **Live video feed** - Plumbers watch the footage in real-time on a monitor
- **High-quality images** - Clear pictures show exactly what's happening inside your pipes

## The Inspection Process: Step by Step

Here's what happens during a sewer camera inspection:

### 1. Finding the Access Point

The technician locates a cleanout or drain opening. This is where the camera enters your sewer line.

### 2. Inserting the Camera

The flexible rod goes into the pipe. It can bend around corners without getting stuck.

### 3. Moving Through the Pipes

The technician guides the camera through your entire sewer system. They check every section for problems.

### 4. Recording What They Find

The camera captures video and photos. This footage shows:

- Blockages
- Cracks
- Root damage
- Pipe damage

### 5. Reviewing the Results

After the inspection, the technician reviews all footage. They explain what they found and recommend solutions.

**The best part?** No digging required. You get answers without tearing up your lawn.

## How Often Should You Schedule Inspections?

The right schedule depends on your home. Consider these factors:

### Building Age

| Home Age | Recommended Frequency |
|----------|----------------------|
| Less than 10 years | Every 3-5 years |
| 10-25 years | Every 2-3 years |
| Over 25 years | Every 1-2 years |

### Past Plumbing Problems

If you've had sewer issues before, inspect more often. Previous problems often signal hidden damage.

### Tree Proximity

Large trees near your sewer line? Schedule yearly inspections. Roots grow toward pipes and can cause major damage.
