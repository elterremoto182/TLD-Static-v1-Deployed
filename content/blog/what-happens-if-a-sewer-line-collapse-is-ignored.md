# What Happens If a Sewer Line Collapse Is Ignored?

If a sewer line collapse is ignored, you risk recurring backups, sewage in the home or yard, and much higher repair costs later. The collapse can worsen and block the line completely. Understanding the risks helps you act before the problem escalates.

## Consequences of Ignoring a Collapse

A sewer line collapse means a section of the pipe has caved in or failed, so wastewater cannot flow through normally. If you ignore it, backups can become more frequent and severe—toilets, sinks, and showers may back up, and sewage can overflow into the home or yard. The collapse can extend as surrounding soil and pipe continue to fail, so the blocked section grows and repair becomes more extensive. Health risks from exposure to sewage increase, and the cost of repair—especially emergency repair—rises. In some cases, the line may need to be dug up and replaced instead of a smaller spot repair. Addressing the problem early, when you first notice slow drains or backups, allows you to confirm the cause with a [professional sewer camera inspection](/sewer-camera-inspection/) and plan repair before the collapse worsens.

## Early Signs You Should Not Ignore

Slow drains, gurgling when you flush, or sewage odor in the yard or basement can be early signs of a failing or collapsing line. If one backup is followed by more, or if multiple fixtures are affected, the problem is likely in the main line. Scheduling a [sewer camera inspection](/sewer-camera-inspection/) at that stage lets you see whether the cause is [pipe collapse](/problems/pipe-collapse/), [root intrusion](/problems/root-intrusion/), or [sewer blockage](/problems/sewer-blockage/) and plan repair before a full collapse or emergency. Ignoring early signs often leads to a complete blockage, sewage backup into the home, and emergency repair at a higher cost.
